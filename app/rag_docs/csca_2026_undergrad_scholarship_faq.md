# CSCA (China Scholastic Competency Assessment) + Undergraduate Scholarship FAQ (2026/2027 intake)

Last updated: 2025-12 (use sources section for verification)

## What is CSCA (in one line)
CSCA is a standardized entrance assessment for international students applying to undergraduate (Bachelor) programs in China and is used as a key reference for admissions and scholarship evaluation for the 2026/2027 academic year.

---

## Fast Answers (for short, engaging replies)

### Q1) What is CSCA and what kind of scholarships do they offer?
**Answer (short):**
CSCA is an entrance assessment used for **undergraduate admissions**. Scholarships are typically under the **Chinese Government Scholarship (CSC)** and other scholarship routes (university/provincial). CSCA itself is an assessment requirement/score report used in evaluation rather than a “separate scholarship provider”.

**Important note for agent:**
Avoid claiming “CSCA gives scholarships directly.” Say “CSCA score is required/used for evaluation in scholarship admissions”.

### Q2) Is CSCA mandatory?
**Answer (short):**
For the **2026/2027 academic year**, international students applying to **undergraduate programs at Chinese Government Scholarship universities** must include a CSCA score report as part of the required materials. (Some English-taught applicants may be exempt from Professional Chinese, but still follow the university’s rules.)

### Q3) Which degree levels does CSCA apply to?
**Answer (short):**
CSCA is focused on **Bachelor/undergraduate** applicants. Master’s/PhD requirements usually rely on degree transcripts + language tests + university rules.

## 1) How to apply (process) for undergraduate scholarship cases where CSCA is required

### Q: What is the application process for CSCA scholarships?
**Answer:**
1) Choose your target university/major and confirm required CSCA subjects for that major.  
2) Register for CSCA at the official portal and take the exam.  
3) Prepare scholarship + university application documents (including CSCA score report).  
4) Submit scholarship/university application by the target deadline, upload required materials, and follow university/authority instructions.

**Agent phrasing tip:** keep it short; then ask 1 lead question like “Which major + September 2026 intake?”

### Q: How early should students apply to be considered for the next academic year?
**Answer:**
Plan early so you can **take CSCA before scholarship/university deadlines**. For September 2026 intake, official notices mention CSCA exam options in **Dec 2025** and **Jan 2026**, and regular sessions in 2026.

## 2) CSCA exam schedule, frequency, format, and scoring

### Q: When is CSCA held?
**Answer:**
- First global CSCA test: **Dec 21, 2025**
- Another stated option for Sep 2026 applicants: **Jan 25, 2026**
- Starting 2026: CSCA is expected **five times per year** (Jan, Mar, Apr, Jun, Dec).

### Q: What subjects are in CSCA?
**Answer:**
- Professional Chinese (Humanities Chinese OR STEM Chinese)
- Mathematics
- Physics
- Chemistry  
(Universities may require different combinations depending on the major.)

### Q: What is the format & score range?
**Answer:**
Common official descriptions state **multiple-choice** format and a **0–100** score range per subject.

### Q: What language are the tests in?
**Answer:**
Professional Chinese is in Chinese; Mathematics/Physics/Chemistry are offered in **Chinese or English**, and candidates choose based on university/major requirements.

## 3) Fees: scholarship application fee vs CSCA exam fee

### Q: Is there an application fee for CSCA scholarships?
**Answer:**
Scholarship application fees depend on the scholarship route/university system.  
**Separate from scholarship fees**, CSCA is an exam and candidates should expect to **pay CSCA exam/registration fees** as required by the official portal.

**Agent rule:** Don’t guess exact amounts unless the retrieved RAG chunk explicitly states a number.

## 4) Documents required (with CSCA/CSC/Chinese Goverment Scholarship-specific items)

### Q: What documents are required?
**Answer (core list):**
Typical required materials include:
- Passport copy
- Highest education certificate + transcripts
- Language proficiency proof (HSK for Chinese-taught, IELTS/TOEFL or other proof for English-taught, depending on university)
- CSCA Score Report (for undergraduate scholarship-linked applications where required)
- Study plan (commonly required; length varies by degree level)
- Recommendation letters (commonly required for graduate; some routes may request them)

### Q: Do students need proof of acceptance / pre-admission document before applying?
**Answer:**
Some scholarship routes require a **pre-admission document** (pre-admission notice/invitation letter) issued by the target university.

## 5) Eligibility (general)

### Q: Are there age restrictions? GPA requirements? nationality restrictions?
**Answer:**
Eligibility varies by scholarship route and country channel. General requirements often include:
- Non-Chinese citizenship, good health
- Academic qualifications for undergraduate entry
- Some channels may include age limits and nationality-specific procedures  
**Agent rule:** If user asks “exact age limit”, reply: “It depends on the scholarship channel + your country’s announcement; share your nationality and target intake and we’ll confirm the current requirement.”

### Q: Is prior Chinese required? Can students apply in English?
**Answer:**
Both routes exist:
- Chinese-taught: usually needs Chinese proficiency proof (e.g., HSK) depending on program rules
- English-taught: many universities accept English proficiency documents; some may exempt Professional Chinese
